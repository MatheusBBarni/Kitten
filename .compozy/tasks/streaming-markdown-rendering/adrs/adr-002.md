# ADR-002: PRD Product Approach - Preview-Deep, Concentrate V1 on the Hand-off Review Console

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-09

## Context

The idea (`_idea.md`) and ADR-001 committed V1 to "elevate Markdown as trustworthy instrumentation for the hand-off," within an envelope that allowed a cheap-80%-everywhere ambient elevation plus a preview center of gravity.
PRD brainstorming then settled three business decisions: the primary user is the hand-off reviewer, the delivery sequence is foundation then wedge then polish, and the primary success signal is a task-based comprehension study on the preview.

Three product approaches were presented: (A) table-stakes ambient plus a differentiated preview, (B) preview-deep - minimal ambient work with nearly all weight on the preview as a rich review console, and (C) a broad ambient-quality release.
The team selected Approach B.

## Decision

V1 concentrates almost all product investment on the hand-off preview as a rich, trustworthy review console, and holds ambient rendering to the minimum needed for correctness.

The minimal foundation is the shared `<Markdown>` seam, the `markup.*` theme scopes so nothing renders flat wherever Markdown already renders, and the #807 compiled-binary highlighting fix so code and diffs highlight in the shipped binary.
All further V1 weight goes to the preview: rendering the summary as Markdown in read state, provenance links from the summary to referenced files, sharper redaction clarity, tighter integration of the kept/dropped files with the pending diff, and clean-copy fidelity.

Success is graded primarily by a task-based comprehension study comparing forward-decision accuracy and speed on the rendered versus the current unrendered preview.
Broad ambient rendering polish beyond "not flat," and any attempt to beat toad on ambient render quality, are out of scope for V1.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Approach A - Table-stakes ambient plus differentiated preview

- **Description**: Elevate rendering everywhere as table stakes, then concentrate weight and measured success on the preview.
- **Pros**: Clears the broken-field bar on every surface; feels finished for evaluators; still wedge-weighted.
- **Cons**: Spreads scarce effort across ambient surfaces that do not move the wedge metric; slower to a deep preview signal.
- **Why rejected**: The team judged the everyday transcript legible enough once the free theme fix lands, and preferred to spend the entire differentiating budget on the wedge surface.

### Alternative 2: Approach C - Ambient-quality release

- **Description**: Treat V1 primarily as a broad rendering-quality release to beat the field and approach toad; the preview is one surface among many.
- **Pros**: Broadest visible polish; best first impression for evaluators.
- **Cons**: Fights toad on its strongest axis via the riggable preference metric the council demoted; underinvests in the hand-off.
- **Why rejected**: Contradicts the instrumentation-for-the-hand-off thesis and the hand-off-reviewer primary-user decision.

## Consequences

### Positive

- The most product-critical surface - the last safety gate before a task leaves for the other agent - becomes legible and trustworthy.
- A clean causal success signal via the comprehension study, rather than a riggable competitor comparison.
- The differentiating investment is concentrated where toad has no answer.
- Minimal ambient scope keeps V1 small and the pre-1.0 OpenTUI risk contained.

### Negative

- The everyday transcript reader gets only the free theme fix, not deeper ambient polish.
- Evaluators comparing ambient render quality against toad see parity, not a decisive lead.
- The differentiated value is intermittent - visible only at hand-off time.

### Risks

- Over-indexing on one surface if hand-off frequency is low; mitigated because the hand-off is the product's core action and the idea's separate hand-off-adoption metrics cover frequency.
- A soft comprehension signal if the study is loosely designed; mitigated by a pre-registered task set, a blind rendered-vs-unrendered comparison, and N >= 20 participants.
- #807 still gates code and diff highlighting inside the preview and must be fixed even though ambient scope is minimal.

## Implementation Notes

This refines ADR-001 rather than contradicting it: it selects the narrower, deeper end of the scope ADR-001 permitted.
The theme fix still lights up the transcript for free because it lives in the shared seam.
