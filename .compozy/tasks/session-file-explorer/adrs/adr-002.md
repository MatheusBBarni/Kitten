# ADR-002: Validate repeat multi-session use before expanding the explorer

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-16

## Context

The Session-Scoped File Explorer is intended to keep developers in Kitten's active agent workflow when they need to inspect and open a workspace file. The feature could grow into a broader workspace surface, but that would compete with established editors and delay the core product learning: whether developers who work across live sessions repeatedly choose Kitten's session-aware browse-and-open flow.

The initial beta should serve daily multi-session Kitten users because they can evaluate the feature's differentiating property: each focused session retains its own workspace context. The product needs one decisive expansion signal rather than a collection of novelty, reliability, or satisfaction signals that can obscure actual habit formation.

## Decision

Launch the complete, safety-bounded browse-and-open workflow to a defined beta of daily multi-session users. Make repeat use across active sessions the primary gate for further investment: the feature earns Phase 2 only when at least 60% of the opt-in beta cohort uses it in three or more active sessions within 14 days, alongside the baseline user-trust and launch-dispatch targets in the idea.

The MVP remains an external-editor bridge, not an embedded editor. Discoverability, saved preference, navigation, and visible outcomes support the inspect-and-open moment; search, previews, file mutation, and broader workspace curation remain deferred until the repeat-use hypothesis is validated.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Expand immediately into an embedded workspace environment

- **Description**: Add editing, previews, search, file changes, and richer workspace actions as part of the initial release.
- **Pros**: A more comprehensive workspace offering and more visible feature breadth.
- **Cons**: Blurs Kitten's relationship to the developer's established editor, delays learning about the core continuity value, and raises the cost of reversal.
- **Why rejected**: The product should validate one focused habit before attempting to compete with mature editor workflows.

### Alternative 2: Gate expansion on first-use discovery or launch reliability alone

- **Description**: Treat first explorer open, successful launcher dispatch, or a positive first-use rating as sufficient evidence.
- **Pros**: Faster-to-collect data and a simpler early report.
- **Cons**: Measures discoverability or operational correctness, not whether the workflow becomes part of regular multi-session work.
- **Why rejected**: Reliability and sentiment remain supporting measures; repeat use is the clearest signal of enduring workflow value.

### Alternative 3: Recruit primarily single-session or new Kitten users

- **Description**: Validate the explorer with users who have only one active session or limited familiarity with Kitten.
- **Pros**: Easier recruitment and a broad usability perspective.
- **Cons**: Weak evidence for the session-aware differentiation that the feature is designed to test.
- **Why rejected**: Daily multi-session users experience the target problem most directly and provide the highest-signal early feedback.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Keeps the MVP aligned with Kitten's live, session-aware agent workflow.
- Creates a clear, measurable decision point before investing in adjacent workspace features.
- Uses a beta audience that can test the product's distinct value rather than generic file-navigation familiarity.

### Negative

- The initial audience is narrower than all Kitten users.
- Advanced workspace requests may wait even when individual beta participants want them.
- A positive reliability result without repeat use will not justify expansion.

### Risks

- **Novelty is mistaken for habit**: require the 14-day, three-session repeat-use threshold and pair it with qualitative continuity feedback.
- **The right users are unavailable for beta**: recruit from active multi-session users first and record cohort limitations before interpreting results.
- **Users expect an embedded editor**: describe the explorer consistently as a bridge to the user's established editor in product copy and onboarding.

## Implementation Notes

The later TechSpec will translate this product decision into telemetry boundaries, discoverability behavior, and rollout instrumentation without widening the MVP's user-facing scope.
