# ADR-002: Reserve MVP questions for consequential operator decisions

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-14

## Context

Kitten needs a consistent way for eligible agent sessions to ask the supervising developer for input. Questions create valuable control at moments of uncertainty, but they also interrupt focused work. The product must therefore distinguish meaningful decisions from routine ambiguity, make the outcome of non-response clear, and prove the value of the experience before expanding it.

Research shows that interactive clarification is an expected coding-agent behavior, but developer trust, privacy, and interruption cost remain material adoption concerns. The approved idea also establishes a provider-neutral, local-first direction with Codex as the end-to-end proof path.

## Decision

For MVP, Kitten will present a high-priority structured question only when an agent reaches a consequential decision where proceeding incorrectly could create an incorrect or irreversible change. The operator receives clear session attribution, concise context, structured choices, a custom-answer path, and an explicit skip path.

If the operator does not respond, the agent receives a transparent timed-out outcome and chooses its safe next step; silence never implies approval. Every eligible configured session receives the same product experience, with Codex serving as the proof path. MVP expansion is earned when at least 80% of eligible questions receive a submitted answer.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Ask for every detected ambiguity

- **Description**: Surface any uncertainty to the operator.
- **Pros**: Maximizes human control and may prevent more mistakes.
- **Cons**: Creates alert fatigue, slows routine work, and makes the cockpit feel interrupt-driven.
- **Why rejected**: The user prioritizes avoiding consequential mistakes, not supervising every minor agent choice.

### Alternative 2: Limit MVP to Codex sessions

- **Description**: Offer the experience only in the first proven provider path.
- **Pros**: Produces a smaller initial launch surface.
- **Cons**: Fragments the multi-provider cockpit and contradicts the provider-neutral value proposition.
- **Why rejected**: The selected MVP promise is one consistent experience for every eligible configured session.

### Alternative 3: Use a non-blocking notification

- **Description**: Let questions wait in the background until the operator opens them.
- **Pros**: Reduces immediate interruption.
- **Cons**: Risks an agent silently waiting while the operator assumes the run is progressing.
- **Why rejected**: Consequential questions must be visible and actionable at the moment they matter.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Gives operators a clear, trustworthy intervention point before meaningful mistakes occur.
- Preserves agent momentum for routine choices.
- Makes no-response behavior explicit and measurable.
- Provides a single product promise across eligible sessions.

### Negative

- Some users may want broader questioning than MVP permits.
- Agents need clear product guidance on what qualifies as consequential.
- High-priority attention can feel disruptive if questions are poorly phrased.

### Risks

- **Interruption fatigue**: limit MVP to consequential decisions and measure submitted-answer rate.
- **Low trust in question handling**: make session attribution and privacy boundaries visible and consistent.
- **Low response rate**: use transparent timeouts and use the 80% target to decide whether to expand.

## Implementation Notes

Detailed behavior and technical delivery belong in the TechSpec. This ADR defines the user-facing decision policy, attention model, rollout promise, and MVP success gate.
