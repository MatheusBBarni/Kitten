# ADR-002: Launch Context Packs as a verified-provider pilot for trusted focused handoffs

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-16

## Context

Context Packs address a cockpit developer's need to give the next agent focused, task-specific context without repeating repository discovery. The approved idea defines a complete review-first product: an operator curates a draft, inspects the exact payload, seals it, and explicitly uses it for an eligible recipient.

The first product decision is how broadly to introduce that promise. The primary operator outcome is a focused handoff. The leading success signal is trusted completion: operators can review and seal useful packs without an unexpected delivery or integrity surprise. The initial audience should be limited to users whose configured sessions can meet the product's stated eligibility checks.

## Decision

Launch Context Packs as a verified-provider pilot focused on trusted, reviewed handoffs. The pilot experience must make availability, source scope, freshness, review status, and delivery choice understandable before an operator commits a pack. It must never imply that an unavailable or ineligible destination is ready for use.

The PRD will retain the full Context Packs product contract as the V1 destination. It will phase delivery by user value and demonstrated trust: first a complete reviewed handoff for eligible pilot users, then durable reuse and additional handoff choices, then the remaining explicitly chosen recipient and export outcomes. A later phase activates only after the preceding phase meets its adoption and trust criteria.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Release Context Packs to all users immediately

- **Description**: Present the feature broadly regardless of whether every configured session can support the stated product experience.
- **Pros**: Maximum immediate reach and faster feedback volume.
- **Cons**: Creates inconsistent availability and risks teaching users that the review-first promise is conditional or unreliable.
- **Why rejected**: Trust is the primary success signal; broad availability before consistent eligibility would dilute the product's value.

### Alternative 2: Permanently limit the product to an ephemeral current-session handoff

- **Description**: Offer a small, one-time context preparation flow with no durable reuse or additional recipient choices.
- **Pros**: Fastest path to a simple operator workflow.
- **Cons**: Omits the portable sealed artifact and the explicit reuse outcomes that distinguish Context Packs from ordinary file attachment.
- **Why rejected**: It is a useful first phase but not the chosen V1 product destination.

### Alternative 3: Start with a shared workspace context library

- **Description**: Prioritize named, shareable, reusable context collections across teams.
- **Pros**: Could create broader collaboration value.
- **Cons**: Adds organization, sharing, and lifecycle concerns before the individual focused-handoff workflow is proven.
- **Why rejected**: It solves a later collaboration opportunity rather than the primary cockpit-developer problem.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Aligns the first release with the selected outcome: a focused handoff the operator can trust.
- Gives pilot users clear expectations rather than exposing partial or inconsistent availability.
- Preserves a measurable path from trusted use to durable reuse and broader delivery choices.

### Negative

- Initial reach is intentionally limited.
- Some operators will see an unavailable state until their sessions meet pilot eligibility.
- The full product outcome arrives through phases rather than in one broad launch.

### Risks

- **Pilot adoption is too low to measure** — recruit design-partner operators and collect only opt-in, content-free funnel outcomes.
- **Review feels slower than manual handoff** — keep the first journey focused on the highest-signal context and measure preparation time alongside trust.
- **Operators misunderstand a sealed pack as permanently current** — show freshness and recipient eligibility plainly at every use decision.
- **A later phase expands before trust is established** — require the preceding phase's completion, integrity, and feedback targets before widening access.

## Implementation Notes

- The PRD defines user-visible phases and their advancement criteria, not an engineering implementation sequence.
- Product copy must distinguish a reviewed sealed pack, a stale pack requiring attention, and an unavailable recipient without implying a workaround.
