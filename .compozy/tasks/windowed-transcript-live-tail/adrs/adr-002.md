# ADR-002: Launch bounded live history as a truth-first experiment

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-15

## Context

Long-session developers need a responsive conversation surface without losing confidence that earlier work remains available. Research shows that coding-agent users expect continuity and reliable history navigation, while Kitten deliberately does not persist transcript content across restarts. The approved idea establishes a bounded live view; PRD clarification selected a hidden-turn count with a clear history-loading action, an explicitly opt-in first release, and graduation only after both reliable behavior and demonstrated responsiveness in long sessions.

## Decision

Launch the bounded live-history experience as a clearly described, default-off experiment. When enabled, it will show a count of older hidden turns and an explicit action to reveal them, protect active work from collapse, preserve the user's reading position while history is revealed, and honestly state that complete history is available during the live run rather than as a cross-restart archive. Promote it to the default experience only after the defined preservation, interaction-stability, and long-session responsiveness evidence is met.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Enable bounded history for every user immediately

- **Description**: Make the bounded view the default without an experimental rollout.
- **Pros**: Fastest broad exposure and no discoverability burden.
- **Cons**: A confusing history interaction would affect users precisely during their longest and most consequential sessions.
- **Why rejected**: The user selected proof of reliable behavior and responsiveness before default adoption.

### Alternative 2: Show a minimal history marker without a count

- **Description**: Collapse older activity behind a generic marker that only offers expansion.
- **Pros**: Minimal visual treatment.
- **Cons**: Gives users little confidence about what remains available or how much context is hidden.
- **Why rejected**: The selected experience requires a visible hidden-turn count to make the state understandable.

### Alternative 3: Build a persistent searchable history product first

- **Description**: Couple bounded presentation with durable transcript storage, search, and cross-restart access.
- **Pros**: Richer continuity and retrieval.
- **Cons**: Changes privacy expectations and delays validation of the immediate long-session problem.
- **Why rejected**: It exceeds the focused live-run value proposition and requires a separate product decision.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Gives long-session developers a clear, low-risk way to validate a more responsive conversation experience.
- Makes older live-run work visibly available rather than silently omitted.
- Creates evidence-based criteria for default adoption.

### Negative

- Early users must discover and opt into the experiment.
- The experience must explain its live-run scope clearly enough to avoid an expectation of restart persistence.

### Risks

- **Users mistake collapsed history for deleted history**: show a count and an explicit reveal action.
- **Users lose their place while reviewing history**: keep their reading position stable and avoid forced return to live activity.
- **Experiment remains enabled without enough value**: require the agreed reliability and responsiveness evidence before broader rollout.

## Implementation Notes

Preserve the approved V1 boundaries: complete history during the live run, no disk transcript archive, no search product, and no configurable policy surface.
