# ADR-002: Position statusline colors as a personal scanability experiment

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-20

## Context

Solo power users repeatedly consult the Kitten statusline while coding, but its
configured fields currently have the same visual weight. The approved idea and
research show that foreground styling is familiar in terminal tools, while many
alternatives require scripts or a broad configuration system. User
clarification confirmed that V1 should optimize faster visual scanning, serve
solo power users first, honor a confirmed chosen color in the current preview,
and use voluntary adoption feedback rather than new telemetry to decide future
investment.

The product must improve recognition without reframing the personal statusline
as a team convention or general theming platform. Existing statusline safety
and confirmation expectations remain product requirements.

## Decision

Deliver optional, per-field foreground color as a narrowly scoped personal
scanability feature. Users can distinguish the existing status information they
already value, review the exact result before saving, and retain normal
theme-derived presentation when they do not choose a color. V1 success is
measured through voluntary evidence that users scan the footer faster, not
through new telemetry.

Keep the feature personal and field-only. Do not broaden V1 into shared
profiles, visual themes, or a manual styling surface. Treat requests for those
capabilities as evidence for a later product decision, not implied scope.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Broader statusline theming

- **Description**: Offer palettes, backgrounds, separator styling, profiles,
  and richer visual controls from the first release.
- **Pros**: Gives users a more expressive personalization surface.
- **Cons**: Dilutes the scanability hypothesis, increases user choice, and
  creates expectations of a long-lived theming product.
- **Why rejected**: The immediate user need is field recognition, not general
  visual composition. The broader product should require separately observed
  demand.

### Alternative 2: Curated visual presets only

- **Description**: Offer a fixed small set of field appearances instead of
  letting users choose a color.
- **Pros**: Reduces configuration choice and provides a uniform experience.
- **Cons**: Does not let a user distinguish the status information that matters
  most in their own workflow.
- **Why rejected**: It weakens the central personal scanability outcome without
  materially improving the product's evidence or safety position.

### Alternative 3: Defer customization

- **Description**: Preserve the current uniform statusline until stronger
  demand is available.
- **Pros**: Avoids near-term documentation and support work.
- **Cons**: Leaves a recurrent, low-cost recognition problem unsolved for the
  target user.
- **Why rejected**: A constrained personal feature is an appropriate, reversible
  way to validate the demand signal.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Users can make the most important existing statusline value easier to scan.
- The product can validate a concrete personalization hypothesis without
  collecting new telemetry.
- The narrow scope keeps product messaging and documentation clear.

### Negative

- Some users will request visual capabilities that V1 deliberately excludes.
- Fixed user-selected colors may look different after a theme or terminal
  change, so users remain responsible for choices they confirm.

### Risks

- **Weak adoption evidence**: collect voluntary feedback for 60 days and avoid
  expansion without a meaningful response set.
- **Misunderstood scope**: communicate that this is a personal,
  foreground-only scanability feature, not a themes or profile system.
- **Readability concerns**: show the current preview before saving and retain
  theme-derived defaults for users who do not select a color.

## Implementation Notes

- Preserve the existing preview-before-save and explicit-confirmation user
  journey.
- Preserve the single personal layout scope and all existing statusline fields.
- Maintain the separate ADR-001 product boundary for canonical input and
  field-only color behavior.
