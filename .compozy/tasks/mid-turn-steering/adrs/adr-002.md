# ADR-002: Make V1 Steering Lossless and Composer-First

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-15

## Context

Developers supervising a long-running agent need to redirect work without losing their instruction or misreading the task's status. Research shows that comparable products distinguish steering from stopping and make queued direction visible. The approved idea established safe steering as a strategic reliability foundation; PRD clarification further established that V1 must restore unsent text visibly, show live state at the composer, and prioritize losslessness over the fastest possible redirect.

## Decision

V1 will provide a single, lossless steering experience for an active task. The composer is the primary live-status surface and shows queued, sending, and failed states. If Kitten cannot safely deliver a steering instruction, it restores the exact text to the composer and tells the developer what happened. The product promises one active task and ordered direction, rather than competing prompts or opaque retries.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Automatically retry failed steering

- **Description**: Retain a message in the background and retry it without further developer action.
- **Pros**: Reduces immediate manual recovery work.
- **Cons**: Can deliver stale or duplicate direction after the developer has changed course.
- **Why rejected**: A visible restored draft gives the developer control over ambiguous delivery.

### Alternative 2: Use a transcript timeline as the primary status surface

- **Description**: Surface all live queue and delivery states as persistent conversation entries.
- **Pros**: Provides detailed historical visibility.
- **Cons**: Adds noise to active work and makes the immediate recovery state harder to find.
- **Why rejected**: Developers selected compact composer-first visibility for the active decision point.

### Alternative 3: Optimize for the fastest interruption

- **Description**: Send or interrupt immediately even when safe delivery confirmation is incomplete.
- **Pros**: Minimizes perceived latency.
- **Cons**: Risks lost, duplicated, or misapplied direction.
- **Why rejected**: The product's value is trustworthy supervision, so losslessness takes priority over speed.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Gives developers a clear recovery path whenever steering cannot be delivered.
- Keeps live intervention status close to the text the developer may need to edit and resend.
- Establishes a differentiated promise of safe, ordered developer control.

### Negative

- Some steering actions may visibly wait while Kitten preserves safe task boundaries.
- Detailed historical intervention controls are deferred.

### Risks

- **Developers expect instant redirection** — explain queued and sending states plainly and preserve their text.
- **Developers confuse steering with stopping** — use distinct language and controls for each action.
- **Status is missed in a busy terminal** — keep the composer state concise, persistent, and accessible without color alone.

## Implementation Notes

V1 excludes automatic retry, a full intervention timeline, provider-policy controls, and multi-user steering. Future phases may add richer history or optimized provider behavior only if the lossless core experience meets its success metrics.
