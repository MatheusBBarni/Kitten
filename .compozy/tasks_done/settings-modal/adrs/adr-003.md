# ADR-003: Include 1-2 named theme presets in the V1 first ship

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-09

## Context

ADR-001 scoped the theme category to `auto / light / dark` for the first ship and explicitly deferred "named or custom theme presets beyond auto/light/dark," on the grounds that a palette registry is more than the first ship needs to prove the override path.
During PRD clarification the product owner chose to include 1-2 named presets (for example a Catppuccin-flavored palette) in V1 to signal the theme-ecosystem angle at launch.
This reverses that one deferral and is recorded here so the change is traceable.

Market context supports the choice: theme ecosystems travel across tools (Catppuccin has 200+ ports; top editor themes exceed 10 million installs), and a recognizable preset name is a stronger adoption signal than three unnamed modes.

## Decision

V1 ships `auto`, `light`, `dark`, plus 1-2 named presets.
This requires introducing a small palette registry, since today only two palettes (dark, light) exist and the resolver only branches on terminal mode.
The registry stays deliberately small in V1 (the built-in modes plus 1-2 presets); arbitrary user-defined custom palettes remain out of scope.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: auto / light / dark only (the ADR-001 default)

- **Description**: Ship just the three modes; presets come in a later phase.
- **Pros**: Smallest possible scope; no palette registry needed in V1.
- **Cons**: The first public increment is pure parity with the terminal's own light/dark; no ecosystem signal at launch.
- **Why rejected**: The product owner judged a named preset worth the modest extra scope for its adoption and differentiation signal.

### Alternative 2: Full custom themes from day one

- **Description**: Let users define arbitrary palettes via JSON or an in-app editor.
- **Pros**: Highest ceiling; complete theming freedom.
- **Cons**: A validation and editing surface well beyond a Quick-Win V1.
- **Why rejected**: Clearly outside the first ship; remains a later-phase candidate.

## Consequences

### Positive

- A recognizable preset gives V1 a concrete "wow" beyond terminal parity.
- Building a small registry now makes adding future presets cheap.

### Negative

- Adds a palette registry to the first ship that ADR-001 had deferred, modestly enlarging V1.
- Named presets must satisfy the existing no-hard-coded-color and legibility constraints across terminal backgrounds, which takes design care per preset.

### Risks

- Preset palettes may read poorly on some terminal backgrounds; mitigated by keeping the preset count to 1-2 in V1 and reviewing each against light and dark backgrounds.

## Implementation Notes

- Extend the palette resolver from a two-constant light/dark branch to a small keyed registry, consulted by the persisted theme preference ahead of the terminal-reported mode.
- Keep the syntax-highlight style keyed by the effective palette, not just the terminal mode.
