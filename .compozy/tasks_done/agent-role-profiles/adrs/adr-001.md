# ADR-001: Fail Closed with an Attestable Fixed Explore Profile

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-15

## Context

Issue [#13](https://github.com/MatheusBBarni/Kitten/issues/13) makes role and capability boundaries a production-readiness dependency for the Kitten-owned orchestration registry and the planned agent-control surface. The primary user is a Kitten operator who delegates work to a child agent. They need an `explore` child whose safety is real and visible, rather than a role label that silently inherits the parent's authority.

The existing delegation path is flat and host-owned, but it clones the parent provider configuration. Prompt wording, hidden tools, or permission interception cannot prove that an unrestricted adapter is read-only. Unbounded concurrent launches also permit resource races. The decision must preserve a trustworthy V1 safety promise while avoiding a broad policy platform before enforcement is proven.

## Decision

V1 will offer one fixed, host-owned `explore` profile. Kitten may launch it only when a runtime capability manifest proves the required restrictions are current and enforceable: read-only execution, no child spawning, no external MCP or agent-control capability, and finite per-parent and global child reservations. Kitten will enforce the policy at advertisement and execution boundaries, reserve limits atomically, expose the effective runtime-scoped policy to the operator, and reject any launch that lacks proof. Unsupported runtimes receive an explicit denial, never a best-effort or warning-only launch.

The initial manifest may support only a safe subset of runtimes. It defines explicit capabilities and negative checks now, while broader provider-neutral policy abstraction, editable role profiles, and workspace overrides remain deferred.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: General editable role-profile system

- **Description**: Ship `explore`, `engineer`, `pair`, and `design` with workspace overrides and custom capability combinations.
- **Pros**: Broad flexibility and a future-oriented configuration surface.
- **Cons**: Creates many policy owners and combinations before containment is proven; expands testing and bypass risk.
- **Why rejected**: It conflicts with the user-selected Minimal MVP and weakens the production-safety gate.

### Alternative 2: Label a child as read-only through prompts, hidden tools, or permission interception

- **Description**: Reuse the inherited provider session and present a restricted role in the UI.
- **Pros**: Fast to implement and available across more runtimes.
- **Cons**: Does not stop direct hidden-tool calls or adapters that run with unrestricted host access.
- **Why rejected**: It is not an enforceable security boundary and would create safety theater.

### Alternative 3: Wait for universal multi-provider capability parity

- **Description**: Delay the feature until every supported provider offers identical verifiable restrictions.
- **Pros**: A uniform product contract from the first release.
- **Cons**: Withholds a proven-safe subset, delays learning, and encourages an abstract contract before real interoperability constraints exist.
- **Why rejected**: A narrow, prominently runtime-scoped capability allowlist can deliver safe operator value now without weakening guarantees.

## Consequences

### Positive

- The `explore` promise is backed by a runtime proof and deterministic denial path.
- Production enablement of agent-control can depend on a concrete least-privilege boundary.
- A fixed profile, atomic reservations, and content-free decision telemetry limit blast radius and provide evidence for future provider support.

### Negative

- Some operators cannot launch `explore` until their runtime has a verified restriction contract.
- V1 needs compatibility evidence, negative tests, and a clear operator explanation for denied launches.
- Broader roles and customization are intentionally delayed.

### Risks

- **Stale or incomplete runtime proof**: pin the tested compatibility envelope, revalidate on runtime/config changes, and fail closed when evidence is absent or stale.
- **Resource-cap races**: reserve and release in the host-owned registry/store path, then test concurrent launch attempts.
- **Operator workarounds**: provide clear denials without bypass guidance and record only allowlisted, content-free decision counters.

## Implementation Notes

Treat policy proof as a launch precondition, not a UI convention. The later technical specification must define the capability manifest, compatibility validation, direct-handler execution checks, policy snapshot, and atomic accounting in detail.
