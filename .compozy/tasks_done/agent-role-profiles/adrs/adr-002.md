# ADR-002: Make Verified Safe Delegation the Operator Product Contract

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-15

## Context

Kitten operators need to delegate exploratory work without accidentally creating a child that has broader authority than the task requires. The approved idea establishes that a child marketed as `explore` must be genuinely safe or unavailable. PRD clarification confirmed three product priorities: operators must gain confidence before work begins, an unavailable safe path must explain the missing guarantee plainly without an unsafe fallback, and early success is defined by complete adherence to the safety contract rather than launch volume or provider breadth.

This feature gates production enablement of the planned agent-control surface. The product must preserve the familiar, visible delegation workflow while making its safety status understandable at launch, during active work, and when a request cannot be accepted.

## Decision

The MVP product contract is one fixed `explore` delegation experience. It is available only when Kitten can verify the promised boundary. Eligible operators receive a visible, focusable child marked as `explore` with a concise textual summary of its active restrictions. Ineligible requests create no child and receive a plain-language explanation that safe delegation is unavailable; Kitten offers no warning-only or unrestricted fallback.

The same contract applies to every production child-launch entry point, including the agent-control surface when it is enabled. MVP success prioritizes zero violations of the safe-delegation promise, clear denial comprehension, and bounded child capacity over role variety and universal provider coverage.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Broad configurable role catalog

- **Description**: Launch `explore`, `engineer`, `pair`, and `design` with operator or workspace customization.
- **Pros**: More flexibility and broader delegation choices immediately.
- **Cons**: Dilutes the safety promise, increases user decisions, and creates inconsistent expectations before one role is proven.
- **Why rejected**: The primary operator need is trustworthy safe exploration, not role variety.

### Alternative 2: Warning-only availability with unrestricted fallback

- **Description**: Let operators continue after a warning when safe delegation cannot be verified.
- **Pros**: Maximizes immediate launch availability.
- **Cons**: Contradicts the meaning of `explore`, shifts risk back to the operator, and undermines trust.
- **Why rejected**: The user explicitly chose clear refusal without an unsafe fallback.

### Alternative 3: Delay until every supported runtime offers the same experience

- **Description**: Withhold the product until universal safe-delegation coverage is available.
- **Pros**: Uniform availability from the first release.
- **Cons**: Delays a valuable proven-safe experience and eliminates evidence for prioritizing broader coverage.
- **Why rejected**: A clearly scoped, safe subset is more valuable than waiting for parity, provided denial is explicit and consistent.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Operators can trust that an available `explore` child matches its displayed safety promise.
- Denied requests are understandable before work begins and cannot silently expand authority.
- The feature becomes a clear production-readiness condition for child-agent control.

### Negative

- Some operators will not have an available `explore` path until their runtime is eligible.
- V1 intentionally offers less choice than the original broad role-profile proposal.
- Product messaging and accessibility must make the distinction between unavailable and active safety status unambiguous.

### Risks

- **Perceived loss of availability**: Explain the benefit of refusal and preserve the operator's original task context.
- **Confusion across supported runtimes**: Disclose availability before launch and use consistent terminology everywhere.
- **Pressure to add unsafe bypasses**: Treat the absence of a fallback as a non-negotiable product boundary.
- **Dependency timing**: Keep the scope aligned with the orchestration registry and agent-control production gate.

## Implementation Notes

The later technical specification must ensure the user-visible contract is consistent across all child-launch paths, present before work begins, and never represented solely by color or an unverified label.
